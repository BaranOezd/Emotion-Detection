

"You’re a wizard, Harry," Hagrid said with a tone of finality. Harry stared at him in disbelief. "I’m a what?" he finally managed to stammer. "A wizard, o’ course," said Hagrid. "An’ a thumpin’ good one, I’d say, once you’ve been trained up a bit." Harry’s head was spinning. He had spent eleven years being told he was nothing special, just a burden, an ordinary boy. And now this giant was telling him he belonged to some magical world? He tried to protest. "But I can’t be a wizard," Harry insisted. "I mean, I’m just... Harry. Just Harry." Hagrid let out a deep laugh. "You’ll see," he said, "just wait until you get yer letter and go to Hogwarts. You’ll fit right in."
